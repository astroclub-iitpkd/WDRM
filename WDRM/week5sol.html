Solution 1

The statement:

$$0.5^{0.7^{0.9^{.^{.^{.}}}}}$$ = ?

has the exponents increasing in the form of an arithmetic progression. Hence, let us consider the sub exponent series from 0.9:

$$0.9^{1.1^{1.3^{1.5^{1.7^{.^{.^{.}}}}}}}$$

One can notice that the exponents get larger without a bound,

$$\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} 1.1^{1.3^{1.5^{1.7^{.^{.^{.}}}}}} \rightarrow\infty$$

Hence,

$$\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} 0.9^{1.1^{1.3^{1.5^{1.7^{.^{.^{.}}}}}}} \rightarrow 0 \text{ as } 0.9^\infty \rightarrow 0$$

Then trivially,

$$\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} 0.7^{0.9^{1.1^{1.3^{1.5^{1.7^{.^{.^{.}}}}}}}} \rightarrow 1 \text{ as } 0.7^0 \rightarrow 1$$

Which brings us to the fact that:

$$0.5^{0.7^{0.9^{.^{.^{.}}}}} = 0.5^1 = 0.5$$

Solution 2

The solution for this becomes simpler when bounds are added. We know that \( x>y>z>0 \) and each of them are integers.

As

$$\frac{1}{x}+\frac{2}{y}+\frac{3}{z} = 1 \Rightarrow \frac{3}{z}<1;\frac{2}{y}<\frac{2}{z};\frac{1}{x} <\frac{1}{z}$$

$$\frac{1}{z}+\frac{2}{z}+\frac{3}{z} > 1 \Rightarrow z <6 $$

Hence we have \( 3< z < 6 \)

Case 1 : \( z=4 \)

$$\frac{1}{x}+\frac{2}{y}+\frac{3}{4} = 1 $$

$$\frac{1}{x}+\frac{2}{y}= \frac{1}{4} $$

$$\frac{y+2x}{xy}= \frac{1}{4} $$

$$4y+8x = xy$$

$$4y+8x -xy - 32= -32$$

$$(x-4)(y-8)= 32$$

Now we can construct the solution from the factors of 32.

$$\begin{cases} x-4 = 32,16,8,4,2,1\\ y-8 = \;1,2,4,8,16,32\\ \end{cases}$$

$$\begin{cases} x = 36,20,\xcancel{12,8,6,5}\\ y = \;9,10,\xcancel{12,16,24,40}\\ \end{cases}$$

As per the inequality ignore wherever \(x < y \).

Which gives us our first two pairs \((36,9,4),(20,10,4)\)
