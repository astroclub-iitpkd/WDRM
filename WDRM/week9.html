Weekly Dose of Recreational Mathematics

Week 9

Date: August 4, 2024

Problems

  1. Given a sequence \( a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_n \) of real numbers. For each \( i \) \( (1 \leq i \leq n) \) define \[ d_i = \max \{a_j : 1 \leq j \leq i\} - \min \{a_j : i \leq j \leq n\} \] and let \[ d = \max \{d_i : 1 \leq i \leq n\}. \]
    1. Prove that for arbitrary real numbers \( x_1 \leq x_2 \leq \cdots \leq x_n \), \[ \max \{|x_i - a_i| : 1 \leq i \leq n\} \geq \frac{d}{2} \, (1). \]
    2. Show that there exists a sequence \( x_1 \leq x_2 \leq \cdots \leq x_n \) of real numbers such that we have equality in (1).
  2. Let \( f(x) \) be a polynomial with real coefficients of degree 2. Suppose that for some pairwise distinct real numbers \( a \), \( b \), \( c \) we have \[ f(a) = bc, \quad f(b) = ca, \quad f(c) = ab. \] Determine \( f(a+b+c) \) in terms of \( a, b, c \).
